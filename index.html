Frequently Asked Questions

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Replace your helmet every 5 years or after an impact. Check for cracks, loose straps, or compressed foam—a damaged helmet won’t protect you. Safety first, always wear a reliable helmet!

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding a stored bike, inspect brakes, tires, chain, and fluids. Check for rust, leaks, or loose parts. Test ride it carefully. Regular maintenance ensures safety—never ride without a proper check!

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Always wear a helmet and safety gear. Follow traffic laws, use signals, and stay visible. Ride defensively, check brakes and lights, avoid distractions, and respect pedestrians. Safety first, always stay alert!
